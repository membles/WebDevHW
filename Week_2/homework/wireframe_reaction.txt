Five Important Cosiderations for Wireframing illustrates how issues can arrise when working with a team to design a UX and how to go about reducing these issues as well as to design a better product overall. It lays out steps to take so that before you start really designing, you have an idea of what you're making, you know what the other teammembers want out of it, and you know what your users want out of it. Making sketches and getting early feedback is definitely an integral part of this so that you don't waste time and can work efficiently towards something that everyone will like. Knowing your audience is always important, whether it be the users or the teammembers/coworkers you're presenting your wireframe to.
